Tsunami Safety Guide

Stay safe before, during, and after a tsunami.

What to Do During a Tsunami

  • Move to higher ground immediately if a tsunami warning is issued.
  • Avoid beaches and coastal areas during the warning.
  • If on a boat, head to deeper waters when possible.
  • Follow evacuation orders and guidance from local authorities promptly.
  • Stay tuned to emergency broadcasts for real-time updates.

What Not to Do

  • Do not go to the shore to observe tsunami waves.
  • Do not ignore evacuation instructions or warnings.
  • Avoid driving towards tsunami-affected areas.
  • Refrain from using bridges or roads that may be compromised.

Before a Tsunami

  • Identify tsunami evacuation routes in your area.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, and documents.
  • Install tsunami alert systems or apps for notifications.
  • Secure loose items in your home to reduce damage.

After a Tsunami

  • Return home only when authorities confirm it is safe.
  • Check for injuries and provide first aid as needed.
  • Avoid standing water, which may be contaminated or electrically charged.
  • Inspect your home for structural damage and report hazards like gas leaks.
  • Document damages for insurance claims with photos or videos.
