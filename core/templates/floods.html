Flood Safety Guide

Be aware. Stay safe. Act responsibly.

What to Do During a Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately if flooding is imminent.
  • Stay away from floodwaters. They may be contaminated or electrically charged.
  • If you are driving, do not attempt to cross flooded roads. Turn around and find an alternate route.
  • Follow evacuation orders issued by authorities promptly.
  • Keep children and pets out of the floodwaters.

What Not to Do

  • Do not walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters. Just six inches of moving water can knock you down.
  • Do not use electrical appliances or touch electrical equipment if you are wet or standing in water.
  • Avoid drinking tap water until authorities confirm its safety.
  • Do not ignore warnings or weather updates from official sources.

  • Secure valuables and move them to higher floors or safe locations.
  • Monitor weather forecasts and warnings from local authorities.

After a Flood

  • Return home only when authorities declare it safe.
  • Inspect your home for structural damage and hazards like gas leaks.
  • Dispose of food, water, and medicines that may have been contaminated.
  • Document damages for insurance claims with photos and videos.
